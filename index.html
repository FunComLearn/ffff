History of Computers

Tally Sticks

Abacus

An abacus is mechanical device used to help individuals in performing mathematical computations like arithmetic operations. It was invented in Babylonia in 2400 B.C.

Napiers Bones

It was invented by John Napier in 1614. It allows the user to multiply, divide, and calculate square and cube roots by moving the rods around and placing them in specially constructed board.

Slide Rule

It was invented by William Oughtred in 1622, and was based on Napiers ideas about logarithms. It was used primarily for multiplication, division, roots, logarithms, and trigonometry, but not regularly used for addition and subtraction.

Pascaline

The Pascaline or also called Arithmetic Machine, the first calculator or adding machine to be produced in any quantity and actually used. This was designed and built by the French mathematician-philosopher Blaise Pascal between 1642 and 1644.

Step Reckoner

The step reckoner (or stepped reckoner) was the first calculator that could perform all four arithmetic operations. It was invented by Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz in 1672.

View full image

Jacquard Loom

The Jacquard Loom is a mechanical loom that used interchangeable punch cards to perform automated tasks. It was invented by Joseph Marie Jacquard in 1881.

View full image

Arithmometer

The Arithmometer is a mechanical calculator invented by Thomas de Colmar in 1820. It is the first reliable, useful and commercially successful calculating machine, that could perform the four basic mathematical functions.

Analytical Engine

The Analytical Engine was a proposed mechanical general-purpose computer designed by English mathematician and computer pioneer Charles Babbage.

Scheutzian Calculation Engine

The first printing calculator, invented by Per Georg Scheutz in 1843. This was based on Charles Babbage's difference engine.

Tabulating Machine

The tabulating machine was invented by Herman Hollerith in 1890. It is designed to be used in summarizing information stored in punched cards.

Z1

Z1 is the first programmable computer and was invented by Konrad Zuse in Germany from 1936 to 1938. To program the Z1, the user needs to insert a punch tape into the punch tape reader. The output was also generated through punch tape.

The first electronic digital computing device. It was invented by Professor John Atanasoff and graduate student Clifford Berry at Iowa State University between 1939 and 1942.

Computer Generation

1st Generation Computer (1940 - 1956)

Vacuum tubes were the primary technology of the first computers. Vacuum tubes were widely used in computers.The input of this system was based on punched cards and paper tape; however, the output was displayed on printouts.

View full image

2nd Generation Computer (1956 - 1963)

Generally referred as Second Generation of Computers. transistor technology was used to develop the second generation of computers. the second generation was significantly smaller in size and less computing time than First Generation.

View full image

3rd Generation Computer (1963 - 1971)

Considered as the period of Third Generation of computers. It developed by using the Integrated Circuit (IC) technology. The size of the computers of the third generation was smaller, computing time was lesser and consumed less power .

View full image

4th Generation Computer (1971-1980)

Computers of fourth generation used Very Large Scale Integrated (VLSI) circuits. took advantage of microprocessor (or what we commonly know now as CPU), which made it much more powerful, compact, and affordable.

View full image

5th Generation Computer (1980 - Onward)

The fifth generation of computers is beginning to use AI (artificial intelligence), an exciting technology with many potential applications around the world. Leaps have been made in AI technology and computers, but there is still room for much improvement.

Computer Hardware & Sofware

System Unit

The system unit is the core of the computer. It contains the main components that is responsible for: processing and storing data; and controls all computer functions. This contains the motherboard, CPU, RAM, etc. Even including the case that houses these components.

View full image

Motherboard

The motherboard is the most important part of the computer. It connects all of the parts of the computer together, and is the largest component on most computers.

View full image

CPU

Central Processing Unit (CPU) also called the processor, is the part of a computer that interprets and carries out instructions of a program by performing the basic arithmetic, logical, control and input/output (I/O) operations specified by the instructions.

View full image

Memory

Computer memory is the storage space in the computer, where data is to be processed and instructions required for processing are stored. It can be classified as either internal or external memory.

View full image

Input Devices

Input devices are peripheral devices or accessories that are used as means to generate input for the computer. It lets the user to enter information into the computer (e.g., keyboard, mouse, webcam, etc.).

View full image

Output Devices

Output devices are peripheral devices that can bring forth an output. After the computer completes a computation, it then integrates with the output device to generate the output. The output device converts data into a human readable formatlike music, graphics, text, audio, and/or video.

Application software, or Apps as we usually call it now, is what we mostly engage ourselves with when we use computers. These types of software are which help us perform tasks. It is also what makes computer popular and easy to use.

System Software

System software is a collection of software that is essential in managing the computer system. It is designed to operate, control and expand the processing power of a computer. System software also makes it easier for hardware and applications to interact and work together.

Computer Malware

Worm

A worm virus is a malicious, self-replicating program that can spread throughout a network without human assistance.

View full image

Trojan

A Trojan horse or Trojan is a type of malware that is often disguised as legitimate software. Trojans can be employed by cyber-thieves and hackers trying to gain access to users' systems. Users are typically tricked by some form of social engineering into loading and executing Trojans on their systems.

View full image

Spyware

Malicious software designed to gain access to or damage your computer, often without your knowledge. Spyware gathers your personal information and relays it to advertisers, data firms, or external users. Spyware is used for many purposes.

View full image

Adware

Advertising-supported malware, is a term used to describe unwanted software that displays advertisements on your device. An adware virus is considered a PUP (potentially unwanted program), which means it's a program that is installed without permission from the user.

View full image

Ransomware

Ransomware is a type of malware from cryptovirology that threatens to publish the victim's data or perpetually block access to it unless a ransom is paid.

Virus

A computer virus is a type of computer program that, when executed, replicates itself by modifying other computer programs and inserting its own code.

Wabbit (1974)

A self-replicating program that made multiple copies of itself on the computer until it degraded the system to the extent that system performance degrades and eventually crashes. Researchers have named the virus a "wabbit" because of the speed it was able to replicate.

Elk Cloner (1982)

Written by a 15-year-old, elk cloner is one of the earliest widespread, self-replicating viruses affecting personal computers. The elk cloner displayed a friendly little poem on the infected system: "It will be found on all your disks; it will infiltrate your chips; yes, it is a cloner!"

It is generally considered to be the first virus to infect MS-DOS computers. It originates from two brothers in Pakistan who built it to test flaws in their company's software.

The authors of malware disguised one of the most popular Trojans as "PC-Writer", a popular shareware program. Once on the system, it will erase all files of the user.

Morris Worm (1988)

The worm infected a large percentage of computers connected to the Internet's precursor ARPANET, essentially bringing the network to its knees within 24 hours. Its release marked a new dawn for malicious software. The author, Robert Morris, became the first malware author convicted for his crimes.

Generally accepted as the first mass-email virus, Melissa used Outlook address books from infected machines, and mailed only 50 people at a time.

It infected an estimated 50 million computers, spreading through emails sent with subject lines. The losses caused major corporations and government bodies, including parts of the Pentagon Parliament, to shut down their email servers. The worm caused more than $ 5.5 billion in damage.

Emails spread this dangerous virus, which has very attractive female tennis players, but is actually hidden malware.
